## Title
Driftstone DP Path

## Concepts Involved
Tabulation, Multi-constraint State, Optimization DP

## Problem Statement
A robot is crossing a line of driftstones.

There are `n` stones numbered from `1` to `n`.
Each stone `i` has a height `height[i]`.
The robot starts on stone `1` and wants to reach stone `n`.

From any stone, the robot may normally jump:
- `1` stone ahead, or
- `2` stones ahead.

The energy cost of a normal jump from stone `i` to stone `j` is:

`absolute difference between height[i] and height[j]`

There are two extra rules:

1. If the robot makes two consecutive `2`-stone jumps, an extra fatigue penalty `p` is added to the second consecutive `2`-stone jump.
2. The robot may use one special `3`-stone jump at most once in the whole journey. The cost of this special jump is double the normal height-difference cost.

Find the minimum total energy required to reach stone `n`.

## Input Format
- First line: integer `n`
- Second line: `n` space-separated integers `height[1], height[2], ..., height[n]`
- Third line: integer `p`

## Output Format
- Print the minimum total energy required to reach stone `n`

## Constraints
- `1 <= n <= 200000`
- `0 <= height[i] <= 1000000000`
- `0 <= p <= 1000000000`

## Examples
### Example 1
Input:
`5`
`10 30 40 20 50`
`15`

Output:
`40`

### Example 2
Input:
`4`
`5 100 5 100`
`50`

Output:
`95`

### Example 3
Input:
`6`
`1 100 2 100 3 4`
`10`

Output:
`5`

## Edge-Case Thinking Prompts
- What if `n = 1`?
- What if the special `3`-stone jump is never useful?
- What if two consecutive `2`-stone jumps are still cheaper even after penalty?
- Does a `3`-stone jump count as a `2`-stone jump for fatigue?
- What information must be remembered after every jump?

## Thinking Like a Programmer
- What choices are available from each stone?
- Which previous decision affects the cost of a new `2`-stone jump?
- How do we remember whether the special `3`-stone jump has already been used?
- Why is stone index alone not enough as a DP state?
- Where should the final minimum answer be collected?
