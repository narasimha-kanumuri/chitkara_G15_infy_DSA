## Title
Coupon Bottom-Up

## Concepts Involved
Tabulation, State = (Index, Coupon Used), Optimization DP

## Problem Statement
A delivery agent climbs a toll staircase with `n` paid steps.

The steps are numbered from `1` to `n`.
Each step `i` has a toll value `cost[i]`.
The delivery agent starts on the ground before step `1` and wants to reach the position just beyond step `n`.

From any position, the agent may move:
- `1` step forward, or
- `2` steps forward.

A toll is paid only when the agent lands on a real step from `1` to `n`.
No toll is paid for the ground or for the final position beyond step `n`.

The agent has one coupon.
The coupon may be used at most once, and it can cancel the toll of exactly one real landed step.
The coupon cannot be used on the ground or on the final position beyond the staircase.

Find the minimum total toll required to move beyond the last step.

## Input Format
- First line: integer `n`
- Second line: `n` space-separated integers `cost[1], cost[2], ..., cost[n]`

## Output Format
- Print the minimum total toll required to move beyond the last step

## Constraints
- `1 <= n <= 200000`
- `0 <= cost[i] <= 1000000000`

## Examples
### Example 1
Input:
`1`
`9`

Output:
`0`

### Example 2
Input:
`4`
`4 1 6 3`

Output:
`1`

### Example 3
Input:
`5`
`2 7 1 4 6`

Output:
`3`

## Edge-Case Thinking Prompts
- What if `n = 1`?
- Can the agent jump directly beyond the only step?
- Is the coupon always useful?
- Can the best path skip the most expensive step?
- Should the coupon be allowed on the virtual finish position?

## Thinking Like a Programmer
- What does it mean to reach a position with the coupon unused?
- What does it mean to reach a position after the coupon has already been used?
- Why is one minimum-cost array not enough?
- How do we represent the final position beyond the last step?
- Where should the final answer be taken from?
