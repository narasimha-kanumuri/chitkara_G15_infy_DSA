# Day 4 = DP II — Take / Skip DP

## problem.md

### Title

- Community Garden Rewards

### Concepts Involved

- Dynamic Programming, Take / Skip Pattern, Optimization DP

### Problem Statement

- A city is developing a line of community garden plots.
- Each plot provides a reward value represented by reward[i].
- The city cannot maintain two adjacent plots at the same time because neighboring maintenance teams interfere with each other.
- However, some plots may have negative rewards because maintaining them costs more than the benefit they generate.
- Find the maximum total reward that can be obtained.
- The city may choose to maintain no plot if that produces a better total reward.

---

### Input Format

- Integer n
- Array reward[] of length n

### Output Format

- Return the maximum reward obtainable

### Constraints

- 1 <= n <= 2 * 10^5
- -10^9 <= reward[i] <= 10^9

---

## Examples

### Example 1

- **Input:**

```text
reward = [3, 8, 4, 9, 2]
```

- **Output:**

```text
17
```

- **Explanation:**
- Choose plots with rewards 8 and 9.
- Total reward = 17.

### Example 2

- **Input:**

```text
reward = [10, 1, 1, 10]
```

- **Output:**

```text
20
```

- **Explanation:**
- Choose first and last plots.
- Total reward = 20.

### Example 3

- **Input:**

```text
reward = [-5, -2, -7]
```

- **Output:**

```text
0
```

- **Explanation:**
- Maintaining no plot is better.

---

## Edge-Case Thinking Prompts

- What if every reward is negative?
- What if only one plot exists?
- Why can a locally large reward sometimes produce a worse final answer?
- What information from previous plots is actually needed?

---

## Thinking Like a Programmer

- At each plot, should we maintain it or skip it?
- If we maintain the current plot, which previous plots remain compatible?
- Can the answer for a large prefix be built from smaller prefixes?
- Is greedily choosing the largest reward always correct?
